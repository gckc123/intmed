Regression analysis

The table below shows the estimates from the key regression models for the mediation analysis.

The mediator models indicated that the exposure variable, supervision, was
  • significantly associated with the mediator, peer, IRR = 1.215, 95% CI = (1.100, 1.341).
  • significantly associated with the mediator, conflict, b = 0.336, 95% CI = (0.217, 0.456).
After accounting for the effects of the mediators , the outcome model indicated that the treatment variable, supervision, was significantly associated with heavyalcohol, IRR = 1.129, 95% CI = (1.006, 1.267).
The outcome model also indicated that
  • peer is significantly associated with the outcome, heavyalcohol, IRR = 1.146, 95% CI = (1.104, 1.190).
  • conflict is not significantly associated with the outcome, heavyalcohol, IRR = 1.009, 95% CI = (0.936, 1.088).

Mediation analysis

Mediation analysis was performed based on the counter-factual framework and the interventional effect (Vansteelandt and Daniel, 2017; Chan and Leung, 2020). The analysis was conducted in R using the intmed package (Chan and Leung, 2020).
Results from the mediation analysis indicated that
  • the indirect effect throughpeerwasstatistically significant, p = 0.002; 33.030% of the total effect was mediated through peer.
  • the indirect effect throughconflictwasnon-signifcant, p = 0.819; 1.684% of the total effect was mediated through conflict.
  • the indirect effect through the dependence between mediators was non-signifcant, p = 0.99872.
  • the indirect effect through the interaction between mediators was non-signifcant, p = 0.819.
  • the direct effect was statistically significant, p = 0.040.
  • the total effect was statistically significant, p = 0.002.
