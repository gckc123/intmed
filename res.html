Mediation analysis

Mediation analysis was performed based on the counter-factual framework and the interventional effect (Vansteelandt and Daniel, 2017; Chan and Leung, 2020). The analysis was conducted in R using the intmed package (Chan and Leung, 2020).
Results from the mediation analysis indicated that
  • the indirect effect through peer2 was statistically significant, p = 0.000; 38.532% of the total effect was mediated through peer2.
  • the indirect effect through supervision was non-signifcant, p = 0.221; 2.624% of the total effect was mediated through supervision.
  • the indirect effect through the dependence between mediators was non-signifcant, p = 0.996.
  • the indirect effect through the interaction between mediators was non-signifcant, p = 0.410.
  • the direct effect was statistically significant, p = 0.000.
  • the total effect was statistically significant, p = 0.000.
