  • The outcome variable, y, was modelled with regression
  • The mediator, m1, was modelled with regression
  • The mediator, m2, was modelled with regression
  • The mediator, m3, was modelled with regression
The mediator models indicated that the exposure variable, x, was
  • significantly associated with the mediator, m1, b = 0.2, 95% CI = ( 0.17, 0.23).
  • significantly associated with the mediator, m2, b = 0.31, 95% CI = ( 0.28, 0.34).
  • significantly associated with the mediator, m3, b = 0.40, 95% CI = ( 0.37, 0.43).

  • m1 is significantly associated with the outcome, y, b = 0.52, 95% CI = ( 0.49, 0.55).

Mediation analysis

Mediation analysis was performed based on the counter-factual framework and the interventional effect (Vansteelandt and Daniel, 2017; Chan and Leung, 2020). The analysis was conducted in R using the intmed package (Chan and Leung, 2020).
Results from the mediation analysis indicated that
  • the indirect effect through m1 was statistically significant, p = 0.000; 7.56% of the total effect was mediated through m1.
  • the indirect effect through m2 was statistically significant, p = 0.000; 13.37% of the total effect was mediated through m2.
  • the indirect effect through m3 was statistically significant, p = 0.000; 20.29% of the total effect was mediated through m3.
  • the direct effect was statistically significant, p = 0.000.
  • the total effect was statistically significant, p = 0.000.
