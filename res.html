The table below shows the estimates from the key regression models for the mediation analysis.

  • significantly associated with the mediator, peer2_factor, OR = 11.66196, 95% CI = (7.45618, 18.24007).
  • significantly associated with the mediator, supervision, b = 0.12061, 95% CI = (0.03950, 0.20172).

Mediation analysis

Mediation analysis was performed based on the counter-factual framework and the interventional effect (Vansteelandt and Daniel, 2017; Chan and Leung, 2020). The analysis was conducted in R using the intmed package (Chan and Leung, 2020).
Results from the mediation analysis indicated that
  • the indirect effect through peer2_factor was statistically significant, p = 0.000; 57.98927% of the total effect was mediated through peer2_factor.
  • the indirect effect through supervision was non-signifcant, p = 0.313; 1.83378% of the total effect was mediated through supervision.
  • the indirect effect through the dependence between mediators was non-signifcant, p = 0.9346.
  • the indirect effect through the interaction between mediators was non-signifcant, p = 0.313.
  • the direct effect was non-signifcant, p = 0.060.
  • the total effect was statistically significant, p = 0.000.
