Mediation analysis was performed based on the counter-factual framework and the interventional effect (Vansteelandt and Daniel, 2017; Chan and Leung, 2020). The analysis was conducted in R using the intmed package (Chan and Leung, 2020). The table below shows the estimates from the key regression models for the mediation analysis.

The mediator models indicated that the exposure variable, supervision, was
  • significantly associated with the mediator, peer, IRR = 1.22, 95% CI = (1.10, 1.35).
  • significantly associated with the mediator, conflict, b = 0.33, 95% CI = (0.21, 0.46).
After accounting for the effects of the mediators , the outcome model indicated that the treatment variable, supervision, was significantly associated with heavyalcohol, IRR = 1.12, 95% CI = (1.00, 1.26).
The outcome model also indicated that
  • peer is significantly associated with the outcome, heavyalcohol, IRR = 1.15, 95% CI = (1.11, 1.19).
  • conflict is not significantly associated with the outcome, heavyalcohol, IRR = 1.01, 95% CI = (0.94, 1.09).
