Regression analysis

The table below shows the estimates from the key regression models for the mediation analysis.
  • The outcome variable, sub_misuse, was modelled with logistic regression
  • The mediator, dev_peer, was modelled with logistic regression
  • The mediator, sub_exp, was modelled with logistic regression
The mediator models indicated that the exposure variable, fam_int, was
  • significantly associated with the mediator, dev_peer, OR = 0.51753, 95% CI = (0.35992, 0.74415).
  • significantly associated with the mediator, sub_exp, OR = 0.54737, 95% CI = (0.37316, 0.80291).
After accounting for the effect of the mediators , the outcome model indicated that the treatment variable, fam_int, was not significantly associated with sub_misuse, OR = 0.60980, 95% CI = (0.33365, 1.11450).
The outcome model also indicated that
  • dev_peer is significantly associated with the outcome, sub_misuse, OR = 4.22160, 95% CI = (1.91002, 9.33071).
  • sub_exp is not significantly associated with the outcome, sub_misuse, OR = 1.68168, 95% CI = (0.75628, 3.73942).
The effect of conflict and gender were adjusted for in the above analyses.

Mediation analysis

Mediation analysis was performed based on the counter-factual framework and the interventional effect (Vansteelandt and Daniel, 2017; Chan and Leung, 2020). The analysis was conducted in R using the intmed package (Chan and Leung, 2020).
Results from the mediation analysis indicated that
  • the indirect effect through dev_peer was statistically significant, p = 0.004; 22.56436% of the total effect was mediated through dev_peer.
  • the indirect effect through sub_exp was non-signifcant, p = 0.206; 7.17372% of the total effect was mediated through sub_exp.
  • the indirect effect through the dependence between mediators was non-signifcant, p = 0.980.
  • the indirect effect through the interaction between mediators was non-signifcant, p = 0.457.
  • the direct effect was non-signifcant, p = 0.106.
  • the total effect was statistically significant, p = 0.014.
